UFA Analytics Framework (2022-2025)

Emulating professional spatial analytics for the Ultimate Frisbee Association.

The Intersection of Sport and Science

This project began at the intersection of my life as an ultimate frisbee player and my academic path as an aspiring data scientist at UCLA. I’ve always been fascinated by the nuances of the game—the spatial dynamics of disc flight and the tactical decisions that increase the likelihood of winning. As an Applied Mathematics student, I realized that the eye test often doesn’t relay everything the data does. This project was born from a desire to bridge that gap, taking the untapped potential of professional Ultimate Frisbee Association (UFA) statistics and applying the same analytical standards and methods seen in sports like basketball or baseball.

The Technical Core

The technical core of this project involved building a robust data pipeline capable of handling the UFA’s API data across the 2022–2025 seasons. The way the UFA API provides its data is directly from both teams in a single game. This causes a need for extra care, as each side records the stats more useful for itself, and not for the other team.

For example, while on defense, that team's stat taker will not record the passes made by the other team. In order to bring the data together into a functional game state dataframe for each game analyzed, I had to construct a pipeline that merged the away and home dataframes, eliminating duplicate events accurately in order to preserve data integrity.

Additionally, by augmenting the provided stats, I engineered specific spatial and temporal features to capture the "flow" of a game, including vertical and horizontal yardage for every throw.

This pipeline transformed thousands of individual events into a clean, game-flow-centric dataset, providing a granular look at how discs move across the field.

From Raw Data to Visual Insights

Once the data was refined, I transitioned the "Master Stats" output into Tableau to bring these metrics to life. The goal was to create a dashboard that wasn't just a collection of charts, but a tool to provide tactical insights for coaches and fans alike. Key visualizations include:

  • Spatial Heatmaps: Mapping completion percentages across different zones of the field to identify high-risk areas.
  • Game Pace Plots: Plotting offensive efficiency vs pass per point to see the correlation between pace and scoring.
  • Dimensional Risk Analysis: Charting scoring percentage and completion percentage versus field location to further understand risk areas.

This presentation serves as the destination of that work—a synthesis of analysis and a love for the sport, packaged into an interactive experience that makes ultimate analytics readily accessible. Finally, this project is in progress. I am looking forward to improving my pipeline and applying machine learning models to continue to advance the analysis done here.

Key Insights

Through feature engineering of vertical yards and stall proxies, I identified several competitive advantages...

  • Gunslinger vs. Valve: A scatter plot analysis of risk vs. execution.
  • The Danger Zone: A spatial heatmap of completion rates across the field.
